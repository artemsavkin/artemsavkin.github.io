How the compute budget is calculated

A video world model is not trained on raw pixels — it is trained on the discrete tokens emitted by a learned tokenizer. The compute budget is therefore driven by the total number of tokens the dataset produces once it passes through that tokenizer's spatio-temporal grid, together with the size of the model.

A single GPU delivers G peak FLOPS, but only a fraction μ — the Model FLOPs Utilization — is realized in practice. Dividing the total training FLOPs by the effective throughput and converting seconds to hours gives the device-hour budget.
